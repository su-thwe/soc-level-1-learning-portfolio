# SOC Level 1 Learning Roadmap

## Purpose

This portfolio tracks my progress while studying SOC Level 1 concepts through TryHackMe and related blue-team practice. My goal is to build a strong foundation for entry-level cybersecurity roles by documenting what I learn in a clear, structured, and practical way.

## Current Focus

I am currently focusing on beginner SOC analyst skills, including:

- Understanding SOC team responsibilities
- Learning how alerts are triaged
- Understanding escalation paths
- Recognizing common attack vectors
- Building defensive investigation habits
- Practicing clear security documentation

## Learning Approach

For each room, I document:

- The main objective of the room
- Key concepts learned
- Why the topic matters to a SOC analyst
- Important tools, terms, or workflows
- My own understanding in simple words
- A small practical takeaway when possible

I avoid documenting room answers, flags, or copied task content. Instead, I focus on what I learned and how it connects to real SOC work.

## Skill Development Goals

- Complete the SOC Level 1 path

### Medium-Term Goals

As this portfolio grows, I want it to support resume points such as:

- Documented SOC Level 1 Rooms covering alert triage, escalation, and blue-team fundamentals

At this stage, I am still building foundational knowledge. The first few rooms are general, so I am treating them as background notes. As the path becomes more technical, I will add more practical investigation notes, screenshots, workflows, and mini-projects.
