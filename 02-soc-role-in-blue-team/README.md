## 1. Objective

This room explained where a SOC Level 1 Analyst fits inside the broader Blue Team. It introduced different defensive security roles, how SOC teams are structured, and how Junior SOC Analysts can grow into more advanced cybersecurity roles.

## 2. Key Concepts Learned

- The Blue Team focuses on defensive security.
- The SOC is usually the first line of defense for monitoring and investigating alerts.
- SOC Level 1 Analysts usually perform initial triage.
- More complex cases are escalated to senior analysts or incident response teams.
- Cybersecurity teams can include SOC, CIRT, GRC, threat intelligence, engineering, and other specialized roles.

## 3. Blue Team Role Map

| Role / Team | Main Focus | Example Responsibility |
|------------|------------|------------------------|
| SOC L1 Analyst | Initial alert triage | Review alerts and decide whether to escalate |
| SOC L2 Analyst | Deeper investigation | Investigate complex or suspicious cases |
| SOC Engineer | Tool maintenance | Configure SIEM, EDR, alerts, and detection tools |
| SOC Manager | Team management | Oversee SOC operations and reporting |
| CIRT | Incident response | Handle urgent or serious incidents |
| GRC Team | Governance, risk, compliance | Policies, audits, and regulatory requirements |
| Red Team | Offensive testing | Simulate attacks to find weaknesses |

## 4. SOC vs CIRT

My understanding:

- SOC = monitoring, detection, triage, escalation, usual blue team
- CIRT = Handlers of serious incidents

## 5. Career Path Understanding

From a SOC Level 1 Analyst and one can later build toward the following roles:

- SOC Level 2 Analyst
- Incident Responder
- Threat Hunter
- Detection Engineer
- SOC Engineer
- Threat Intelligence Analyst
- Digital Forensics Analyst
